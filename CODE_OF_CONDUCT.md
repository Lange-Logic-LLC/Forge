# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Forge is a community of contributors, maintainers, and users working together to build a trustworthy open-source mobile build platform. We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, productive, and welcoming experience for everyone — regardless of background, experience level, identity, or perspective.

## Our Standards

We expect everyone participating in Forge spaces (the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any official channels) to:

- Treat others with respect, courtesy, and good faith.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the project and its users.
- Assume good intent, while recognizing the impact of our words and actions on others.
- Acknowledge mistakes, apologize when warranted, and learn from the experience.

Conduct that has a negative impact on the community — including personal attacks, discriminatory or derogatory comments, unwelcome attention, intimidation, doxxing, or any behavior that a reasonable person would consider unprofessional — is not acceptable.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Representing the project includes using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Adoption of the Contributor Covenant

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) as its baseline standard of conduct. In the event of any conflict or ambiguity between this document and the Contributor Covenant, the Contributor Covenant governs. The full text is available at:

**https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/**

## Reporting

If you experience or witness conduct that violates this Code, please report it to the maintainers at:

- **Email:** `conduct@langelogic.com`

Reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All reports will be handled with discretion. In your report, please include:

- Your contact information (if you wish to be contacted for follow-up).
- The names of any individuals involved (including witnesses, if any).
- A description of what happened, including where and when.
- Any supporting context (links, screenshots, prior interactions) that helps explain the situation.

The maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of anyone who reports an incident in good faith.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Consequences for violations may include, at the maintainers' discretion:

1. **Private conversation** — a direct, private request to correct the behavior, with an explanation of why it was inappropriate.
2. **Public warning** — a public note that the behavior violates the Code, with a request to stop.
3. **Temporary restriction** — temporary removal of the right to participate in project spaces (comments, issues, pull requests) for a defined period.
4. **Permanent removal** — permanent ban from all project spaces.

The specific response will be proportionate to the severity and pattern of the behavior. The full enforcement guidelines from the Contributor Covenant 2.1 — which this project follows — are available at the canonical URL above.
